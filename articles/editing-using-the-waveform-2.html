Editing using the Waveform 2

This guide shows the current waveform editor in app.alitu.com. You can use it to make manual edits, apply Magic Filters, split clips, add silence or bleeps, and work alongside the transcript.

If you prefer editing by words instead of by waveform, see the separate text-based editing article.

Open the waveform editor

  1. Open your episode in My Episodes.

  2. Find the clip you want to work on and click Edit.

  3. Alitu opens the full editor, with the waveform on the left and the transcript tools on the right.

What you can do from the toolbar

The current toolbar includes these tools:

  1. CUT to remove part of the audio.

  2. MUTE to silence part of the audio without changing the clip length.

  3. BLEEP to cover a section with a bleep sound.

  4. SILENCE to insert silence.

  5. MARKER to drop a marker for later review.

  6. CLIP to export a section as a separate clip.

  7. SPLIT to divide the clip into two separate clips.

  8. FILTERS to run Magic Filters.

  9. BACKGROUND MUSIC to add music from your library, record a layer, or upload a file.

Make a manual cut

  1. Click CUT.

  2. Use the waveform to select the section you want to remove.

  3. Confirm the cut when the edit controls appear.

  4. Use Undo or Redo at the top of the editor if you want to step back or reapply an edit.

Tip: the transcript stays available while you edit, so you can use the words on the right to help you find the exact moment you want on the waveform.

Use Magic Filters

Magic Filters can quickly find common clean-up edits for you.

  1. Click FILTERS.

  2. Choose Unneeded Silences, Filler Words, or both.

  3. Click Apply filters.

  4. Review the suggested edits and keep or remove anything you do not want.

Split a clip

  1. Move the playhead to the point where you want to split the audio.

  2. Click SPLIT.

  3. Check the split position and rename the two new clips if needed.

  4. Click Split.

Adjust editor preferences

Click Preferences at the top of the editor to control how the editor behaves.

  • Choose the waveform look: Simple or Professional.

  • Turn Auto-zoom when making cuts on or off.

  • Turn Keep playhead in center when playing on or off.

Add background music from inside the editor

  1. Click BACKGROUND MUSIC.

  2. Choose music from My Library, Record Audio, or Upload File.

  3. Add the item you want and continue editing.

Playback and transcript tools

At the bottom of the editor you can play, skip back or forward 10 seconds, jump to the start or end, and change playback speed. On the right, you can search the transcript, download the raw transcript, or flag transcript problems.

When you are finished, click Done to return to the episode builder, or Save edited version to Library if you want a separate saved copy.

Best practice

For the most natural result, make your cuts around pauses or quieter parts of speech where possible. If several tiny cuts sound awkward, try one slightly larger cut instead.
