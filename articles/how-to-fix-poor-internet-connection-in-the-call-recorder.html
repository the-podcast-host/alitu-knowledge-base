A solid internet connection is key to enjoying smooth voice calls. In this guide, we'll walk you through some simple steps to improve your internet connection.

1. Speed Check: First things first, check your internet speed. Run a quick speed test using websites like Speedtest (speedtest.net) to ensure you have at least 1 Mbps for both upload and download speeds.

2. Wired Connection: If possible, use an Ethernet cable to connect your device directly to the router. This wired connection is often more stable than Wi-Fi.

3. Reboot Your Router and Modem: Sometimes, your router and modem need a quick reboot to fix temporary issues. Unplug them, wait for 30 seconds, and then plug them back in.

4. Bandwidth-Hungry Devices: Check if other devices on your network are using up a lot of bandwidth, like streaming videos or large downloads. Ask others to ease off while you're on a call.

5. Pause Background Downloads: Automatic updates and cloud backups can hog your bandwidth. Pause or schedule these tasks for non-call hours.

6. Get Closer to the Router: If using Wi-Fi, make sure you're not too far from the router. Walls and distance can weaken the signal.

7. Disconnect Unused Devices: Disconnect or pause internet usage on devices you're not currently using. This frees up more bandwidth for your call.

10. Check for Outages: Sometimes, the issue might be with your internet service provider. Check for known outages in your area or contact your internet provider for information.

11. Try Another Network: If issues persist, switch to a different network, like mobile data or a different Wi-Fi network to see if the problem is specific to your current network.

12. Contact Your ISP: If all else fails, reach out to your Internet Service Provider for assistance and possible upgrades to your plan.

By following these steps, you can resolve most issues related to a poor internet connection during voice calls. A stable internet connection is essential for a seamless calling experience, so addressing these issues will help you stay connected and enjoy your voice calls with fewer interruptions.
