# AI Writing Tropes To Avoid

Use this file only as a final cleanup aid for KB writing.

Do not use it as the main writing prompt.

The main job is still:

- get the product facts right
- shape the article in Alitu's published house style
- use this reference only to remove obvious AI-shaped habits

## What Matters Most For Alitu KB Writing

These are the highest-signal trope checks for this skill.

### 1. Hype and false importance

Avoid language that tries to make a normal help article sound profound, visionary, or dramatic.

Examples to remove:

- `quietly`
- `deeply`
- `fundamentally`
- `remarkably`
- `robust`
- `streamline`
- `harness`
- `landscape`
- `ecosystem`
- `paradigm`

Preferred move:

- replace ornate or inflated wording with plain product-specific wording

### 2. Fake rhetorical drama

Avoid patterns that manufacture suspense instead of helping the reader.

Especially avoid:

- `It's not X. It's Y.`
- `Not X. Not Y. Just Z.`
- `The result? ...`
- `The worst part? ...`
- `Here's the thing.`
- `Here's the kicker.`
- `Here's where it gets interesting.`

Preferred move:

- say the point directly

### 3. Teacher voice and patronizing analogies

Avoid:

- `Let's break this down`
- `Let's unpack this`
- `Let's dive in`
- `Think of it as...`
- `It's like...`

Preferred move:

- explain the feature or task directly unless an analogy genuinely makes it clearer

### 4. Filler transitions

Avoid transitions that add no meaning:

- `It's worth noting`
- `Importantly`
- `Interestingly`
- `Notably`
- `In summary`
- `In conclusion`

Preferred move:

- either make the point directly or cut the sentence

### 5. Structural repetition

Avoid repeated use of:

- anaphora (`They can... They can... They can...`)
- stacked tricolons
- short punchy fragment paragraphs
- listicles disguised as prose
- restating the same point with new metaphors

Preferred move:

- keep one point stated once, clearly

### 6. Weak sourcing language

Avoid vague authority phrases:

- `Experts say`
- `Industry reports suggest`
- `Observers have noted`

Preferred move:

- name the source or remove the claim

### 7. Formatting tells

Avoid:

- em-dash overuse
- bold-first bullets as the default list format
- decorative unicode arrows
- curly-quote-heavy output when plain text is clearer

Preferred move:

- keep formatting plain and functional

## KB-Specific Anti-Trope Reminders

For Alitu KB articles specifically:

- do not let a support article sound like a keynote
- do not let a concept explainer turn into a product-comparison essay
- do not let a troubleshooting article drift into generic blog advice
- do not let screenshots replace the written steps
- do not let a short router page pretend to be a full how-to

## Fast Cleanup Questions

Before finalizing, ask:

1. Is this sentence trying to sound impressive instead of helpful?
2. Did I use a dramatic reframe where a direct sentence would do?
3. Did I repeat the same point in multiple rhetorical shapes?
4. Did I add a transition that can be deleted with no loss?
5. Does this sound like Alitu help content, or like generic AI prose?

If yes, simplify.
